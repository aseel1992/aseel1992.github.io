My name is Aseel Alhomsi and I am a fourth year Telecommunication student at the University of Florida. I was born in Amman, Jordan and I immigrated to the United States at the age of seven. I lived in Tampa, Florida for ten years before moving to Gainesville to attend UF.

After I learned to speak English fluently, I found that I had a talent; one that I may have never discovered had I not moved to America: writing. This made me become interested in Journalism, and I decided to major in this when I began at Santa Fe College. After transferring to the University of Florida, I discovered a broader track in the College of Journalism that focused on the production and means of communication as well as the writing aspect of Journalism: Telecommunication - Media and Society.

Throughout my time as a college student, I have gained many experiences that have taught me life lessons to carry with me forever. One of the most important things that I have learned as I have matured is to always follow my heart. I have recently began to become more and more interested in the physical body and how it works. In the near future, I hope to both further my education in the field of communication, as well as pursue my curiosity and enthusiasm for physical therapy.
